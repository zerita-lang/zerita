# Articles

Zerita has two articles: the definite article ta and the indefinite article une.
Zero and Collective numbers, as well as the abstract class have no articles.
Also, proper nouns don't have articles, only common ones. The article must agree
with the noun on the number.

One key element is that the addition of the article turns proper nouns in common ones,
for example "une adamis" means "a person named George".

## Declension tables

### Definite article

| Number     | Nominative |
| ---------- | ---------- |
| Fractional | tad        |
| Singular   | ta         |
| Plural     | tam        |

### Indefinite article

| Number     | Nominative |
| ---------- | ---------- |
| Fractional | uned       |
| Singular   | une        |
| Plural     | unem       |

## Determinatives

The article isn't mandatory in zerita, but a determinative is mandatory,
unless the noun is in the abstract category, the zero, or the collective number.

## Examples

1.  _convenivo adamon._

    -   **translation:** I met Adam.
    -   **explanation:** Talking about a specific person named Adam that you know.

1.  _convenivo une adamon._

    -   **translation:** I met a guy named Adam.
