# Questions

Questions in zerita are formed by the question particle + the sentence, in the
affirmative syntax, with the unknown part missing. Yes/no questions, in spoken
zerita, have a rising intonation at the end of a sentence.

## Question particles

| Particle | English           | Meaning          |
| -------- | ----------------- | ---------------- |
| ku       |                   | yes/no question  |
| ke       | who/what          | generic question |
| kuál     | which             | choice           |
| kove     | where             | location         |
| kuando   | when              | time             |
| kuót     | how much/how many | quantity         |
| kume     | how               | manner           |

The particles also use the preposition/postposition and declination that is expected.
When "ke" is used to ask about the subject of the verb, the verb matching the number of
ke in the 3rd person. Similarly, "kuanto" causes the time suffix to drop.
Asking how much or how many uses the singular form of the noun.

Interrogative words can be combined with prepositions, for example "pro ke", to ask why
something is happening (purpose), or "por ke" to ask for whose benefit something is happening.

## Examples

1.  _ku donavis ton libron meim?_

    -   **translation:** Are you giving the book to me?

1.  _kume donavis ton libron meim?_

    -   **translation:** How are you giving the book to me? (are you posting it, by hand, etc)

1.  _kove donavis ton libron meim?_

    -   **translation:** Where are you giving the book to me?

1.  _keim donavis ton libron?_

    -   **translation:** To who are you giving the book?

1.  _keon donavis meim?_

    -   **translation:** What are you giving to me?

1.  _kualon libron donavis meim?_

    -   **translation:** Which book are you giving to me?

1.  _keis donaven ton libron meim?_

    -   **translation:** Who is giving the book to me? (The 3rd person is used with who)

1.  _kemis donavem ton libron meim?_

    -   **translation:** Who is giving the book to me? (I know that they are multiple people doing so)

1.  _kuando donovis ton libron meim?_

    -   **translation:** When are you going to give the book to me? (I know it's in the future)

1.  _kuót libron donavis meim?_

    -   **translation:** How many books are you giving to me?

When the event is unknown if it has already happened or it will happen, stabila is used with when:

1.  _kuanto suven tis fiestis?_

    -   **translation:** When is the party? (It might have already happened)

Some common questions include:

1.  _ke seven vei tis namis?_

    -   **translation:** What's your name?

1.  _kume savis?_

    -   **translation:** How are you?

1.  _ex kove sevis?_

    -   **translation:** Where are you from?
